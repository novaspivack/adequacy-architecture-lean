\begin{abstract}
We present a machine-checked layered certificate architecture for outer
admissibility in the adequacy framework.  The central result is that the
finite admissibility gate $\Gadm$ is logically equivalent to the pullback
display witness $\Upb$ for all type-indexed slots $\gamma$
(\emph{Program 1 Unification}).  The unified gate implies the existence of
an absolute frontier S1 certificate and is maximal under the declared OG1
grammar (normal forms NF0--NF3).  The nearest boundary candidate---the NF4
win predicate $\SoneLawful$---is Layer-B sound but does not force grammar
expansion (\emph{NF4-C verdict}).  Above the gate, a functorial second layer
($\Stwo$, the S5\,/\,summit-FE3 package) is canonically produced from any
admission witness without additional hypotheses.  Stronger Stage-D content is
residual: IC-specific, not implied by $\Upb$ alone, and partially
reconstructible from named hooks.  The resulting picture is a strict
three-level architecture---outer gate, functorial second layer, residual
band---every boundary of which is characterized by a named, machine-checked
theorem.  All proofs are in Lean 4 with 0 \texttt{sorry}; exact names and
modules are given in the appendix.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The adequacy architecture defines a class of outer certificate predicates
$C : \mathsf{Type} \to \mathsf{Prop}$ evaluated at a type-indexed slot
$\gamma$.  The fundamental question is: \emph{which predicates $C$ are
genuinely admissible}---meaning they imply the existence of an absolute
frontier S1 representation?

This is not a trivial question.  Naively, any predicate that happens to hold
at some $\gamma$ could be called a ``certificate.''  Program 1 disciplines
this by defining a finite, constructively presented family of admissible outer
modes and proving that all of them collapse to a single unified gate.

The main results of the program, all machine-checked, are:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Roman*)]
  \item \textbf{Unification.} $\Gadm\,\gamma \iff \Upb\,\gamma$ for all $\gamma$.
  \item \textbf{Flagship consequence.} $\Gadm\,\gamma \Rightarrow \exists P\,B,\, \AbsRawSone(P,B)$.
  \item \textbf{Grammar-cut maximality.} Every grammar-generated outer class
    (OG1, NF0--NF3) is contained in $\Upb$.
  \item \textbf{NF4-C boundary.} The nearest expansion candidate
    ($\SoneLawful$) is Layer-B sound but not universally reducible to $\Upb$;
    the grammar boundary is stable.
  \item \textbf{Second layer.} $\Upb\,\gamma \Rightarrow \Stwo\,\gamma$
    (S5\,/\,summit-FE3 pullback-display package), a functorial consequence,
    not a new outer mode.
  \item \textbf{Stage D residuality (SR-B).} $\StageD\,\gamma$ is not implied
    by $\Upb\,\gamma$; it carries a genuine IC-local residue.
\end{enumerate}

The paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:background} establishes
the setting and notation.  Sections~\ref{sec:layer-a}--\ref{sec:layer-c}
present the three layers.  Section~\ref{sec:boundary} discusses the NF4
boundary.  Section~\ref{sec:structure} synthesizes the full architectural
picture.  Section~\ref{sec:open} states the explicitly open frontiers.
The appendix (\S\ref{sec:appendix}) gives exact Lean theorem names, modules,
and the dependency graph.

\section{Setting and notation}
\label{sec:background}

\subsection{The outer certificate world}

Fix a universe of types.  An \emph{outer certificate predicate} is a predicate
$C : \mathsf{Type} \to \mathsf{Prop}$.  We evaluate such predicates at a
type-indexed slot $\gamma$.  The slot $\gamma$ is the outer variable throughout
Program 1.

\begin{definition}[Finite admissibility gate]
  $\Gadm\,\gamma$\, ($\lean{Program1FiniteGAdm}$) holds when $\gamma$ is
  witnessed by one of three constructor arms:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item A \emph{pullback display arm}: a
      $\lean{Program1AdmissibilityPullbackDisplayWitness}\,\gamma$.
    \item An \emph{IC Stage-D full pack arm}: the full
      $\lean{icSpec031\_stageD\_resolution\_pack}$ at $\gamma$.
    \item An \emph{upgrade witness arm}: a
      $\lean{Program1CertifiedUpgradeWitness}\,\gamma$.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Pullback display witness]
  $\Upb\,\gamma$\, ($\lean{Program1AdmissibilityPullbackDisplayWitness}$)
  holds when there is a $\lean{CertifiedFrontierRepresentation}$ (or
  equivalent display witness) obtained by pulling back a lawful adequacy
  architecture along a compare map at $\gamma$.
\end{definition}

\noindent
$\Upb$ is not vacuous: the IC case threads through a compression carrier
pullback proved in $\lean{SPEC\_031\_ZK9}$; the corpus swap case uses an
involutive compare-map cleaving proved in $\lean{SPEC\_035\_UC1}$.

\section{Layer A: The outer gate theorem}
\label{sec:layer-a}

\subsection{Unification}

\begin{theorem}[Program 1 Unification]
  \label{thm:unification}
  For all $\gamma$,\quad $\Gadm\,\gamma \iff \Upb\,\gamma$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{leanbox}
program1FiniteGAdm_iff_program1AdmissibilityPullbackDisplayWitness :
  forall gamma, G_adm gamma <-> U_pullback gamma
-- Module: Instances/Program1MetaUnificationMU1.lean
\end{leanbox}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Each of the three constructor arms of $\Gadm$ is shown to produce a
$\Upb$ witness, and conversely.  The IC arm uses the compression carrier
pullback from $\lean{SPEC\_031\_ZK9}$.  The upgrade arm factors through
$\lean{CertifiedSummitRowRepresentation}$ via the representation-calculus gate
in $\lean{SPEC\_033\_RL9}$.  The pullback arm is definitionally the first
constructor and the conversion is immediate.  The converse direction packages
any $\Upb$ witness into the first constructor arm of $\Gadm$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The nontriviality runs in both directions.  From above: three structurally
different arms collapse to one.  From below: $\Upb$ requires a genuine lawful
adequacy architecture pullback; it is not trivially satisfied.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Flagship consequence}

\begin{theorem}[Absolute frontier S1 from admission]
  \label{thm:flagship}
  For all $\gamma$,\quad $\Gadm\,\gamma \Rightarrow \exists P\,B,\,\AbsRawSone(P,B)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{leanbox}
program1FiniteGAdm_implies_exists_absoluteFrontierRawS1 :
  forall gamma, G_adm gamma -> exists P B, AbsoluteFrontierRawS1 P B
-- Module: Instances/Program1MetaUnificationMU1.lean
\end{leanbox}

\noindent
This is the primary outer payoff: admission at any slot witnesses the
non-emptiness of the absolute frontier.

\subsection{Grammar-cut maximality}

\begin{theorem}[Grammar-cut maximality]
  \label{thm:maximality}
  Let $C : \mathsf{Type} \to \mathsf{Prop}$.  If $C$ is generated by the
  OG1 grammar $(\Ggen\,C)$, then $\forall\,\gamma,\, C\,\gamma \Rightarrow \Upb\,\gamma$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{leanbox}
program1_outer_grammar_cut_maxA :
  forall (C : Type -> Prop),
    Program1GrammarGeneratedOuterGate C ->
    forall gamma, C gamma -> U_pullback gamma
-- Module: Instances/Program1OuterGateGrammar.lean
\end{leanbox}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The OG1 grammar is the closure of the normal forms NF0 (bare pullback
display), NF1 (certified frontier row), NF2 (upgrade witness), NF3
(composition) under the declared grammar rules.  Each normal form is shown to
produce a $\Upb$ witness; the closure induction is straightforward.
\end{proof}

\noindent
Theorem~\ref{thm:maximality} is the correct form of a maximality claim: it
does not assert maximality over all possible outer predicates (that would be
false; see \S\ref{sec:boundary}), but over the disciplined grammar.  Within
that scope, $\Upb$ is the ceiling.

\section{Boundary analysis: the NF4 finding}
\label{sec:boundary}

\subsection{What NF4 is}

Define the \emph{NF4 win predicate}
$\SoneLawful\,\gamma \coloneqq \mathsf{Nonempty}(\lean{S1LawfulFrontierRow}\,\gamma)$.
NF4 is the nearest principled candidate for a broader outer class: it lies
outside the OG1 grammar, but is related to the gate by a soundness result.

\begin{theorem}[NF4 Layer-B soundness]
  \label{thm:nf4-sound}
  For all $\gamma$,\quad $\SoneLawful\,\gamma \Rightarrow \lean{program1OuterLayerBLaw}\,\gamma$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{leanbox}
Program1OuterCertificateSound_program1OuterWinS1LawfulNonempty :
  forall gamma, S1Lawful gamma -> program1OuterLayerBLaw gamma
-- Module: Instances/Program1MaximalityMA1.lean
\end{leanbox}

\subsection{The NF4-C verdict}

The NF4-C verdict records three facts jointly:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\SoneLawful$ is Layer-B sound (Theorem~\ref{thm:nf4-sound}).
  \item $\SoneLawful$ is \emph{not} grammar-generated under OG1 (not in
    NF0--NF3).
  \item There is \emph{no proved universal} $\SoneLawful\,\gamma \Rightarrow
    \Upb\,\gamma$.
\end{enumerate}
NF4 therefore remains a \emph{logical pocket}: sound where instantiated, but
not promoted to the operative outer gate.

\begin{remark}
NF4-C strengthens Theorem~\ref{thm:maximality}: the grammar boundary is not an
artifact of under-specification.  The nearest escape hatch was examined and did
not force expansion.  The architecture is stable at its current boundary.
\end{remark}

\section{Layer B: The functorial second layer}
\label{sec:layer-b}

\begin{theorem}[S5 functorial second layer]
  \label{thm:second-layer}
  \begin{align*}
    &\forall\,\gamma,\quad \Upb\,\gamma \;\Rightarrow\; \Stwo\,\gamma\\
    &\forall\,\gamma,\quad \Gadm\,\gamma \;\Rightarrow\; \Stwo\,\gamma
  \end{align*}
\end{theorem}

\begin{leanbox}
program1_layer2PullbackDisplay_with_host_summitFE3_of_U_pullback :
  forall gamma, U_pullback gamma -> L2 gamma
program1_layer2PullbackDisplay_with_host_summitFE3_of_program1FiniteGAdm :
  forall gamma, G_adm gamma -> L2 gamma
-- Module: Instances/Program1LayeredStructureLS1.lean
\end{leanbox}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The $\Stwo$ package ($\lean{pullbackDisplay\_with\_host\_summitFE3}$) is
obtained by projecting the summit-FE3 witness from the same display structure
that witnesses $\Upb\,\gamma$.  No additional hypotheses are required: the
display witness contains all the data needed to produce the host-side
$\lean{summitFE3}$ certificate.
\end{proof}

\noindent
The structural point is that $\Stwo$ is not a new constructor of $\Gadm$.
It is not a new way to be admitted; it is what admission \emph{unlocks}.
Specifically:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\Stwo\,\gamma$ is not grammar-generated (not in NF0--NF3, not in NF4).
  \item $\Stwo\,\gamma$ does not imply $\Gadm\,\gamma$ (it is downstream).
  \item $\Stwo\,\gamma$ is the unique canonically produced second-layer
    structure from any admission witness.
\end{itemize}
The outer certificate world therefore has a strict two-level structure:
Level~1 is admission; Level~2 is the functorial $\Stwo$ consequence.

\section{Layer C: Stage D as residual content}
\label{sec:layer-c}

\subsection{The Stage D pack}

Stage D is the IC-native condition connecting the IC compression architecture
to the corpus $H_{\mathrm{final}}$.  It has a four-conjunct resolution pack:

\begin{theorem}[Stage D resolution]
  \label{thm:staged-resolution}
  For all $\gamma$,
  \[
    \StageD\,\gamma
    \;\iff\;
    F_1(\gamma) \;\wedge\; F_2(\gamma) \;\wedge\; F_3(\gamma) \;\wedge\; F_4(\gamma)
  \]
  where $F_1$ is the AF1 obstruction conjunct ($\lean{af1\_obstruction\_conjunct}$),
  $F_2$ is the absolute-frontier S1 conjunct
  ($\lean{absoluteFrontierRawS1\_conjunct}$),
  $F_3$ is the bypass conjunct ($\lean{bypass\_conjunct}$),
  and $F_4$ is the cascade conjunct ($\lean{cascade\_conjunct}$).
\end{theorem}

\begin{leanbox}
icSpec031_stageD_resolution_pack :
  forall gamma, StageD gamma <->
    af1_obstruction_conjunct gamma /\
    absoluteFrontierRawS1_conjunct gamma /\
    bypass_conjunct gamma /\
    cascade_conjunct gamma
-- Module: Instances/ProgramFiniteAdmissibility.lean
\end{leanbox}

Named facet hooks (SR1 reconstruction, from \lean{SPEC\_043}):
\begin{leanbox}
icSpec031_stageD_iff_conjunct_of_af1_native_carrier  -- F1: iff spine
icSpec031_stageD_bypass_conjunct_of_af1_standard     -- F3: bypass
icSpec031_stageD_cascade_conjunct_of_af1_standard    -- F4: cascade
\end{leanbox}

\subsection{SR-B residuality verdict}

\begin{proposition}[Stage D residuality]
  \label{prop:stageD-residual}
  \begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
    \item $\Upb\,\gamma$ does not imply $\StageD\,\gamma$ in general.
    \item $\StageD\,\gamma$ does not imply $\Upb\,\gamma$ without the $F_1$
      and $F_3/F_4$ arms.
    \item $F_1$, $F_3$, $F_4$ are reconstructible from named hypotheses
      (partial basis).
    \item $F_2$ (the $\AbsRawSone$ conjunct) is the irreducible residue:
      it does not follow from $\Upb\,\gamma$ alone.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\noindent
Stage D is therefore \emph{residual extra structure}: it sits above the outer
gate and the S5 layer, is partially accessible via named Lean hooks, but
carries a genuine IC-local obstruction that is not reached by $\Upb$ alone.
This is the SR-B verdict from \lean{SPEC\_043\_SR1}.

\section{The full architecture}
\label{sec:structure}

The six results of \S\S\ref{sec:layer-a}--\ref{sec:layer-c} combine into a
single coherent picture.  Every boundary is characterized by what does and
does not follow from $\Upb$ alone.

\subsection{The three-level diagram}

\begin{center}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!5, colframe=gray!40, width=0.88\linewidth,
  arc=3pt, boxrule=0.5pt]
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\small]
  NF4 (S1Lawful)  -- Layer-B sound; not grammar-generated;
                     not universally = U_pullback  [NF4-C]
       ^
  ---------------------------------------------------------
  Residual: Stage D  (IC-local; SR-B; partial basis above
                      U_pullback; irreducible F2)
       ^
  ---------------------------------------------------------
  Level 2: L2  (pullbackDisplay_with_host_summitFE3)
               [functorial from U_pullback; not an outer mode]
       ^
  ---------------------------------------------------------
  Level 1: G_adm <=> U_pullback  (outer gate; maximal NF0--NF3)
       v
  exists P B, AbsoluteFrontierRawS1 P B
\end{Verbatim}
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{center}

\noindent
Every arrow is a named theorem.  The horizontal rules are not informal
``levels'' --- they are precise logical boundaries characterized by what is
and is not derivable from $\Upb$ alone.

\subsection{The integrated claim}

For the disciplined Program-1 certificate world:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*)]
  \item \textbf{Unique unified outer gate.} $\Gadm \equiv \Upb$
    (Theorem~\ref{thm:unification}).
  \item \textbf{Absolute frontier consequence.}
    $\Gadm\,\gamma \Rightarrow \exists P\,B,\,\AbsRawSone(P,B)$
    (Theorem~\ref{thm:flagship}).
  \item \textbf{Grammar maximality.} No OG1-grammar-generated class exceeds
    $\Upb$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:maximality}).
  \item \textbf{Stable boundary.} The nearest expansion candidate (NF4) does
    not force grammar expansion (NF4-C, \S\ref{sec:boundary}).
  \item \textbf{Functorial second layer.} $\Upb\,\gamma \Rightarrow \Stwo\,\gamma$,
    canonically, without additional hypotheses
    (Theorem~\ref{thm:second-layer}).
  \item \textbf{Residual band.} $\StageD$ is not primitive to the outer gate;
    it carries a genuine IC-local residue (Proposition~\ref{prop:stageD-residual}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{What the architecture is not claiming}

\begin{itemize}
  \item It does not claim maximality over \emph{all} possible outer predicates.
    NF4 exists and is not grammar-generated; the maximality result is correctly
    scoped to the OG1 grammar.
  \item It does not claim that $\AbsRawSone$ holds for arbitrary $(P,B)$
    without hypotheses.  Global raw $\forall P\,B$ is the honest post-closure
    frontier per \lean{SPEC\_034} and remains open.
  \item It does not claim that Stage D is unreachable or unimportant.
    SR-B says it has a partial basis and an irreducible residue; it does not
    close off future work on the IC-local spine.
\end{itemize}

\section{Open frontiers}
\label{sec:open}

Three frontiers are explicitly left open:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\Roman*)]
  \item \textbf{Global raw $\forall P\,B$.}
    $\AbsRawSone(P,B)$ for an arbitrary predicate pair without hypotheses.
    This is the honest post-closure frontier per \lean{SPEC\_034\_PC7}.

  \item \textbf{Broader outer grammar.}
    If a principled broader-than-OG1 grammar is chartered in a future program,
    the grammar-cut maximality result would need re-examination in that scope.
    NF4-C is not a proof that no broader grammar is possible; it is a proof
    that the nearest candidate did not force one now.

  \item \textbf{Stage D universality.}
    Whether $\StageD$ could ever be brought inside the outer gate for all
    $\gamma$, or whether the IC-local obstruction (F2) is genuinely permanent.
    The SR-B verdict gives ``partial basis, irreducible residue,'' and does
    not close off future work.
\end{enumerate}

These are not gaps in the current theorem stack.  They are the next genuine
frontiers, now clearly located by the architecture.
